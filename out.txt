6.2 Executive Summary
This report focuses on an Intelligent Drone Delivery Management System with Battery Consumption Prediction and Resource-Aware Multi-hop Route Optimization. The selected topic 
is related to drone-based last-mile delivery in smart logistics, where drones are used to transport packages from warehouses to customers through multiple intermediate hubs.
The real-life business problem is that drone delivery operations are strongly limited by battery capacity, weather conditions, hub congestion, charging availability, and drone readiness. In the current process, delivery planning is often difficult because operators may not know exactly whether a drone has enough battery, which route is most efficient, or when a new delivery order can actually start.
The main process analyzed is the drone delivery planning and execution process, including order creation, hub selection, route planning, drone assignment, battery estimation, delivery tracking, and performance monitoring. Key problems in the current process include inaccurate battery estimation, manual or static route planning, inefficient drone utilization, charging queue bottlenecks, and limited visibility for operators.
The proposed improvement is to apply analytics and machine learning to predict battery consumption for each flight segment, optimize multi-hop routing, assign drones based on availability and battery status, and provide a dashboard for real-time monitoring. This improvement can help reduce delivery delays, improve drone utilization, lower energy consumption, and support better operational decision-making.
6.3 Business Context
The topic is set in the context of a smart logistics or e-commerce delivery organization that uses drones for last-mile delivery. Instead of relying only on traditional vehicles, the organization operates a drone delivery network consisting of warehouses, intermediate hubs, charging stations, drones, operators, technicians, and customers.
The related business activity is the delivery order fulfillment process. When a customer places an order, the system needs to determine whether the delivery can be served, which hub should handle the order, which route should be used, which drone should be assigned for each flight segment, and when the customer can expect to receive the package.
This topic is important in practice because drone delivery can reduce delivery time and improve service coverage, especially for short-distance and urgent deliveries. However, drone operations are more complex than normal delivery because each drone has limited battery life, payload capacity, flight range, and must be affected by weather conditions such as wind speed, wind direction, temperature, humidity, and rainfall.
Data plays a central role in this context. The system uses drone data, battery data, hub status, charging queue data, delivery request data, weather data, and route data to support operational decisions. For example, battery consumption prediction can estimate how much energy a drone will use for each route segment, while hub data can show whether a hub has available drones or charging slots.
The DTA301 analytics result is relevant because it supports the business process through prediction and optimization. Battery consumption prediction helps operators decide whether a drone can complete a flight safely. Multi-hop route optimization helps select the best route across hubs. Drone assignment analytics helps allocate available drones efficiently. As a result, analytics directly improves the delivery planning process, reduces operational risk, and supports faster and more reliable delivery service.
6.4 Business Problem
The current difficulty is that drone delivery planning depends on many changing factors, including battery level, payload weight, route distance, hub workload, charging queue, drone availability, and weather conditions. If these factors are not analyzed properly, the system may choose an inefficient route, assign an unsuitable drone, delay the delivery, or even create safety risks during flight.
This problem affects multiple users, including operators, delivery managers, technicians, customers, and data analysts. Operators need reliable recommendations to plan deliveries. Technicians need to monitor drone battery and maintenance status. Customers need accurate estimated delivery time. Managers need to improve delivery success rate, cost efficiency, and resource utilization.
If the problem is not solved, the organization may face delivery delays, higher energy consumption, low drone utilization, poor customer experience, increased operational cost, and higher safety risk. This is not only a technical or data problem because it directly affects the business process of receiving orders, planning delivery, assigning resources, monitoring execution, and informing customers.
Prediction and optimization can support better decisions by estimating battery consumption, forecasting earliest start time, identifying route risks, and selecting the most suitable drone and hub. Therefore, analytics helps transform the process from manual or rule-based planning into a data-driven delivery management process.

6.8 Proposed Process – TO-BE Process
To address the limitations identified in the current process, the proposed TO-BE process integrates Machine Learning (ML) for battery consumption prediction, a Joint Optimization Engine for route and resource allocation, and a Dynamic Rerouting System for real-time decision making. Unlike the current deterministic approach, the new process utilizes real-time operational data and predictive analytics to improve delivery reliability, resource utilization, and operational efficiency.
The major improvements include:
Replacing distance-based battery estimation with an ML-based battery prediction model.
Automatically evaluating network availability before accepting delivery requests.
Simultaneously optimizing route selection, drone assignment, and hub resource allocation.
Monitoring weather conditions, battery status, and hub congestion in real time.
Automatically triggering dynamic rerouting when operational risks are detected.
Transforming the role of operators from manual planners into system supervisors.
These improvements enable the system to become more efficient, accurate, and controllable while reducing delivery delays and operational risks.

Battery consumption prediction using Machine Learning.

Resource allocation based on charging slot and landing pad availability.
Changes in Roles
In the current process, operators manually perform route planning, battery checking, and drone assignment. In the proposed process, these tasks are automated by intelligent systems. Operators mainly monitor system performance and intervene only in exceptional situations.
Expected Benefits
The proposed process is expected to provide several benefits:
Improved battery prediction accuracy and reduced risk of energy depletion.
Reduced congestion at hubs through optimized resource allocation.
Faster route planning and drone assignment.
More accurate delivery time estimation.
Better utilization of drones, charging stations, and landing pads.
Increased delivery throughput and operational reliability.
Enhanced safety through real-time monitoring and dynamic rerouting.

Process Summary
The proposed AI-driven drone delivery process enhances operational efficiency by integrating predictive analytics and intelligent decision support throughout the delivery lifecycle. The process begins when a customer submits a delivery request. The system automatically evaluates delivery feasibility and estimates the expected delivery time based on current network conditions, weather information, fleet availability, and hub congestion data.
Next, a Machine Learning-based Battery Prediction Engine estimates battery consumption for all feasible delivery routes using factors such as payload weight, flight distance, wind speed, wind direction, and historical battery performance. These prediction results are then provided to a Joint Optimization Engine that simultaneously determines the optimal route, drone assignment, and hub resource allocation plan.

Main Flow

Candidate delivery routes are generated and evaluated using the Machine Learning Battery Prediction Engine. The delivery mission is executed while operational conditions are continuously monitored. After successful delivery, the drone returns to the nearest hub, and operational data are stored for performance analysis and future model improvement.
Alternative Flow
If multiple feasible routes satisfy all operational constraints, the Joint Optimization Engine selects the route with the highest overall efficiency score by considering predicted battery consumption, delivery time, hub congestion, drone availability, and resource utilization. If several drones are available, the system automatically assigns the drone with the highest operational readiness and sufficient battery reserve.
Exception Flow
If the prediction engine determines that no feasible delivery plan exists because of insufficient battery capacity, unavailable drones, adverse weather conditions, or overloaded hubs, the 
delivery request is temporarily rejected or postponed. During mission execution, if battery depletion, unexpected weather changes, or severe hub congestion are detected, the Dynamic Rerouting Engine automatically generates an alternative route. If no safe alternative route is available, the system triggers emergency handling procedures such as mission suspension, emergency landing, or operator intervention.
End Conditions

The delivery is successfully completed and confirmed by the customer.

10.7.3 Notification and Escalation Rules
Situation
Notification / Escalation
Delivery request fails validation (Request Valid? = No)
Customer is automatically notified of rejection with the specific reason (e.g., invalid address, payload exceeds limit); Order Management System logs the rejected record.
Delivery is not feasible (Delivery Feasible? = No)
Customer is notified that the order cannot be fulfilled at this time; Operations Supervisor receives an alert to review resource constraints and determine if the order should be postponed.

Route Planning Service triggers a re-evaluation with relaxed constraints; if no route is found after retry, Operations Supervisor is escalated to manually review or reschedule.

Battery Prediction Service flags all routes as unsafe; Route Planning Service is blocked from proceeding; Operations Supervisor is notified to assess drone fleet readiness or reschedule delivery.

Hub Management System automatically initiates package transfer and assigns the next-hop drone; no manual action is required under normal conditions.

Customer automatically receives a delivery confirmation notification; Order Management System updates the status to Completed; the analytics platform stores all flight and battery records.
